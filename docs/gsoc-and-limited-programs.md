# GSoC and Limited Programs

Incubation makes a project eligible to prepare ideas for OSL programs when
available. It does not guarantee participation. Google Summer of Code and
similar programs have external selection processes and limited contributor
slots. OSL may have to prioritize among many strong project ideas.

## What OSL can do

OSL will do its best to participate in GSoC, internships, grants, and similar
opportunities when possible. When OSL participates and has enough capacity, OSL
may help incubated projects prepare project ideas, clarify scope, and coordinate
review.

## What is not guaranteed

Incubation does not guarantee:

- OSL participation in a specific program year;
- external organization acceptance;
- contributor slots;
- funding;
- interns;
- mentors;
- project idea selection;
- contributor acceptance;
- long-term maintenance by OSL.

Even when OSL participates, not every incubated project idea can be selected or
funded.

## Readiness expectations

Projects that want to participate should provide:

- clear project ideas;
- scoped tasks and expected outcomes;
- public issues or project board links;
- active mentors and backup reviewers;
- communication channels;
- contributor prerequisites;
- review cadence;
- enough maintainer capacity to support contributors.

## Contributor communication

Maintainers and mentors should avoid language that promises acceptance, funding,
employment, or selection. Be clear that final program decisions depend on
external selection, OSL capacity, mentor availability, project readiness, and
limited slots.
